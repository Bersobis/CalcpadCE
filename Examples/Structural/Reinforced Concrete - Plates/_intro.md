[CalcpadCE](https://calcpad-ce.org) worksheets in this section derive the bending moments of two-way [reinforced concrete slabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforced_concrete) on rectangular panels with assorted edge restraint conditions, using the moment coefficients of yield-line theory applied to the short and long span aspect ratio.

The series covers the full set of nine boundary cases that a designer encounters in a typical floor: the [simply supported panel](#simply-supported-slab-panel) and the [fully restrained panel](#fully-restrained-slab-panel) at the extremes, the panels with [one longer edge restrained](#restrained-longer-edge), [one shorter edge restrained](#restrained-shorter-edge), [two adjacent edges restrained](#restrained-adjacent-edges), [both longer edges restrained](#restrained-both-longer-edges) and [both shorter edges restrained](#restrained-both-shorter-edges), and the inverse cases of [a panel with an unrestrained longer edge](#restrained-panel-with-unrestrained-longer-edge) or [an unrestrained shorter edge](#restrained-panel-with-unrestrained-shorter-edge).

Each sheet returns the design moments at the centre of the span and along the restrained edges, and follows up with the required reinforcement areas in both directions for the chosen concrete and steel grades.
