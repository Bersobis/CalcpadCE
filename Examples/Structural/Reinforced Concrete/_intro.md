[CalcpadCE](https://calcpad-ce.org) worksheets in this section handle the verification and design of [reinforced concrete](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforced_concrete) members at ultimate and serviceability limit states according to [Eurocode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurocode_2) EN 1992-1-1 — bending, shear, torsion, axial force and their combinations on rectangular and T-cross-sections.

Bending of beams is treated separately for [rectangular](#bending-design-of-rectangular-section) and [T-sections](#bending-design-of-tee-section), with a dedicated worksheet for the [bending capacity of a T-section](#bending-capacity-of-tee-section) given the reinforcement layout.
[Shear of rectangular sections](#shear-design-of-rectangular-section) covers the variable-strut-inclination model and the maximum strut compression check, and the [combined bending, shear and torsion sheet](#combined-bending-shear-and-torsion) adds the space-truss model for the torsional resistance.

Columns are verified for [uniaxial](#column-design-for-bending-and-axial-force) and [biaxial bending with axial force](#column-design-for-biaxial-bending-and-axial-force), with a parallel [unit-aware variant](#column-design-for-biaxial-bending-and-axial-force-with-units) and a full [N-M interaction diagram](#interaction-diagram-of-rc-section) generated by sweeping the neutral-axis depth.

Serviceability is addressed by stress, crack-width and deflection checks for [rectangular](#sls-design-of-beam-with-rectangular-section) and [T-cross-sections](#sls-design-of-beam-with-tee-section), with the static scheme picked from a drop-down list.
A small [reinforcement-area picker](#areas-of-reinforcement-bars) returns the total area $A_s$ for a chosen bar diameter and count.
